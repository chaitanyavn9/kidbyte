🔍 The Problem

Kids are surrounded by technology
but nobody explains it simply.

Most edtech platforms either skip hardware entirely, use adult vocabulary, or require subscriptions with ads. There's no single destination that goes from "what is a folder" to "what is a pixel" — in language a 6-year-old understands.

😵

Complex Language

Wikipedia and docs use technical terms. Kids bounce off immediately.

💸

Paywalls & Ads

BrainPOP charges subscriptions. Most free sites are littered with ads aimed at children.

🎯

Too Narrow

Scratch covers coding only. CS Unplugged has no interactivity. Nothing covers hardware + OS + binary + pixels all in one place.

02 / 10
💡 The KidByte Approach

Every concept explained as something
the child already knows.

No jargon. No textbooks. Just real-world analogies that click instantly.

Tech Concept KidByte Explains It As…
📁 Folder "A room in your house. Knock twice (double-click) to open the door."
🖱️ Drag & Drop "Like mom twisting your ear and pulling you to sit where she wants."
🧠 CPU "Your brain — it tells every part of the computer what to do."
📚 RAM "Your study table — open books on it while you work. Cleared when you leave."
🎨 GPU "An artist who paints every tiny dot on your screen, at lightning speed."
🔢 Binary "Millions of tiny light switches — ON is 1, OFF is 0. That's all computers speak!"
🖼️ Pixel "A mosaic tile. Millions of tiny coloured tiles make the picture on your screen."

🎬 Interactive Animations

Learn by doing, not reading

Every lesson has a hands-on Framer Motion demo the child interacts with — not just watches.

📁

Knock-Knock Folder

Click the folder once — it shakes. Click again — it opens and reveals files inside. Just like knocking on a door twice.

🧠

CPU Brain Signals

Animated pulses shoot out from the CPU chip to Screen, Sound, Memory, Mouse, Keyboard, Fan — in real time.

📚

RAM Study Table

Open and close apps — watch the RAM bar fill and empty. Too many apps → the table overflows → computer slows!

🔢

Live Binary Converter

Type your name — see each letter converted to 8-bit binary with animated light-switch visualisation.

🖼️

Pixel Resolution Slider

Switch between 4×4, 8×8, 16×16, 32×32. Watch a heart shape go from blocky pixels to sharp and smooth.

🚧

More Coming Soon

RGB colour mixer, drag-and-drop character demo, OS traffic-cop animation, HDD needle writer…

🔒 Privacy Promise

Built for children. Privacy is non-negotiable.

KidByte was designed from day one with one rule: we must never know anything about the child using it.

🚫

Zero Advertisements

No ads. No sponsored content. No affiliate links. The platform is not monetised through attention.

👁️‍🗨️

Zero Tracking

No Google Analytics, no Mixpanel, no Facebook pixel. Not a single tracking script loads.

🍪

Zero Cookies

No cookies set — not even "necessary" ones. No consent banner needed because there is nothing to consent to.

💾

sessionStorage Only

Name, age, avatar, progress — stored only in sessionStorage. Browser close = everything gone. No localStorage. No IndexedDB.

🌐

No Backend Server

There is no server to breach. No database to leak. No API endpoint that could be abused. Fully static files.

📥

Local PDF Generation

The digital badge PDF is generated using jsPDF entirely inside the browser. Nothing is uploaded to generate it.
